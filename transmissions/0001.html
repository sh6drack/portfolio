DRESSED IN SOMEONE ELSE'S MEMORY

there’s not a single version of the world that isn’t dressed in someone else’s memory.

people walk around thinking they’re being seen, but what actually gets seen is a shortcut. a placeholder. a pattern that matched something familiar once.

every interaction is a rerun. new face, same response.

language bends too easily. meaning skips across the surface. but the weight stays underneath. not everything needs to land.
